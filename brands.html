ROLEX

Founded by Hans Wilsdorf in London in 1905, Rolex moved to Geneva in 1919 and has since become the most recognised watch brand in the world. More than a timepiece — it is a crown worn on the wrist.

From the depths of the ocean with the Submariner to the heights of Mount Everest with the Explorer, Rolex watches accompany those who dare to push the boundaries of human achievement.

PATEK
PHILIPPE

The most prestigious watch manufacturer in the world. Patek Philippe has never been sold to a conglomerate and remains family-owned — a fact that speaks to the independence of spirit that defines every timepiece they create.

Their legendary slogan — "You never actually own a Patek Philippe. You merely look after it for the next generation" — is not marketing. It is a philosophy lived in every complication, every movement, every hand-finished component.

Est. 1875 — Le Brassus, Vallée de Joux, Switzerland
AUDEMARS
PIGUET

Founded in the remote Vallée de Joux by two friends, Jules-Louis Audemars and Edward-Auguste Piguet, this maison has remained family-owned for 150 years — a feat unmatched in horology.

In 1972, Gerald Genta designed the Royal Oak — the world's first luxury sports watch in stainless steel. Priced higher than gold watches of the era, it was considered commercial madness. It changed the industry forever.

Est. 1755 — Geneva, Switzerland
VACHERON
CONSTANTIN

With an unbroken history dating to 1755, Vacheron Constantin is the world's oldest continuously operating watch manufacturer. Founded by Jean-Marc Vacheron in Geneva, the maison has never ceased production across wars, economic crises, and revolutions.

Member of the prestigious Richemont Group, Vacheron Constantin holds the coveted Hallmark of Geneva — the highest standard of watchmaking excellence.

Est. 1845 — Glashütte, Saxony, Germany
A. LANGE
& SÖHNE

Founded by Adolf Lange in 1845, the manufacture in Glashütte represents the pinnacle of German horological craft. After the Second World War forced closure and nationalisation, the maison was spectacularly reborn by Walter Lange, Adolf's great-grandson, in 1990.

Every A. Lange & Söhne watch is assembled twice — once to check performance, then disassembled and reassembled with hand-finished, rhodium-plated three-quarter plate movement. This is German precision without compromise.

Est. 1833 — Le Sentier, Switzerland
JAEGER-
LECOULTRE

Known as "The Watchmaker's Watchmaker," Jaeger-LeCoultre has invented over 1,400 calibres — including movements supplied to Patek Philippe, Audemars Piguet, and other great houses. Their Atelier sits in the Vallée de Joux, horology's most hallowed valley.

The iconic Reverso, created in 1931 for polo players who needed protection for their watch crystal, remains one of the most elegant timepieces ever conceived — and a perennial icon of Art Deco design.
