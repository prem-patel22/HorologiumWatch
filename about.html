The Beginning

A Century of Curatorial Excellence

In 1905, master watchmaker Édouard Bertrand walked out of his Geneva atelier with a singular vision: to create an institution that would preserve and share the greatest horological masterworks ever conceived. He had spent two decades working alongside the greats — repairing movements for Patek Philippe, sourcing complications for Vacheron Constantin.

What began as a small, private gallery on the Rue du Rhône became the world's most respected destination for discerning collectors. Kings, industrialists, and cultural luminaries passed through its gilded doors seeking not merely a watch — but a legacy to wear on their wrist.

Today, HOROLOGIUM stands as the definitive authority in luxury timepiece curation — with showrooms on four continents and a reputation built on 120 years of absolute integrity.

The Philosophy

More Than A Watch.
A Declaration

We have always believed that a fine timepiece is not simply a machine for measuring seconds. It is a statement of values — of precision, of patience, of the understanding that the greatest things in life are crafted slowly, carefully, and with absolute intention.

Every piece we offer has been authenticated by our panel of master horologists — each with decades of experience working directly with the world's great manufactures. We accept no compromises in provenance, condition, or service history.

Our collectors trust us not just to find extraordinary watches, but to guard the stories behind them — to ensure that each timepiece passes from one worthy custodian to the next.

Our Process

The HOROLOGIUM
Authentication Standard

Every watch that enters our collection passes through the most rigorous authentication process in the industry. We have spent 120 years perfecting this process — and we continue to refine it as watchmaking technology evolves.

Our team of twelve master horologists brings over 280 combined years of experience working directly with Rolex, Patek Philippe, Audemars Piguet, and the other great houses. No certificate alone suffices — every movement is personally examined.

01

Provenance Investigation

Full documentation review — original sales receipt, service records, and chain of ownership verified against manufacturer archives where possible.

02

External Examination

Case, crystal, crown, bezel, bracelet, and dial examined under 10× magnification. Serial and reference numbers cross-referenced against production records.

03

Movement Analysis

Case opened and movement examined by a specialist in the relevant calibre. Finishing, signatures, and component originality assessed against known examples.

04

Timing Certification

Movement timed across six positions on a certified chronometer tester. Accuracy, amplitude, and beat error recorded and compared to factory specification.

05

HOROLOGIUM Certificate Issued

Successful pieces receive the HOROLOGIUM Certificate of Authenticity — numbered, signed, and permanently registered in our archive. Internationally recognised.
